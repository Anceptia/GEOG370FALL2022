In this project I learned how to display images in different projections

Describe in your own words how you displayed the map in different projections using QGIS

I displayed the map in different projections using QGIS by using the Natural Earth Vector dataset. I used the ne_10m_admin_0_countries layer for each map and used the Indicatrix Mapper to get the Latitude and Longitude and used the Project Propertities (CRS) to import each EPSG map.

WGS84 Projection

The biggest disadvantage of WGS84 is that it appears to be "flat" and is a more difficult setup for map applications. WGS84 is advantageous because it is one of the most commonly used projections and gives a more accurate appearance of the world map

Aitoff Projection

I observed that this map is more rounded and the cicrcles from the graticule are closer together at the top and bottom of the map. An advantage would be is that its scale is correct along the equator and the central meridian. A disadvantage is that its areas, shapes, and distances, and angles are disorted. The area is inaccurate and only best on spheres

Pseudo Mercator

I observed that this map is centered on Greenwich and is more cylindrical. An advantage of this map is that its meridians are vertical lines and are parallel and equally spaced. The graticule is also symmetric across the equator and central meridian. A disadvantage to this map is that its distorted toward the polar regions.

Sphere Winkel I

I observed that this map is centered on Greenwich. An advantage of this map is that the parallels on the Graticule are equally distributed straight lines perpendicular to the central meridian. A disadvantage is that this map is disorted in terms of its areas, distances, directions, and angles. The higher latitude areas are exaggerated and the meridians are more disorted towards the edges of the projection.

World Cylindrical Equal Area

I observed that this map presents the world as more of a rectangular shape. An advantage of this map is that the meridians are vertical lines and parallel to each other and equally spaced. The lines of latitude are horizontal straight lines and are perpendicular to meridians. The graticule is also symmetric across the equator and the central meridian. A disadvantage is that shapes are distorted north and south between the standard parallels.

World Equidistant Conic

I observed that this map is a conic projection map with standard parallels on the northern and southern hemisphere. An advantage of this map is that all the meridians are equally spaced straight and the lines converge to a common point. When parallels are set on the northern hemisphere the fan shape of the graticule is oriented up. When parallels are on the southern hemisphere, the fan shape of the graticule is oriented down. The graticule is also symmetric across the central meridian. A disadvantage of this map is that shapes, areas, distances, directions, and angles are distorted.

North Pole Azimuthal Equidistant

I observed that this map is centered on the North Pole and is a Azimuthal projection map. An advantage of this map is that the graticule is symmetric and all parallels are equally spaced along the central meridian. The graticule is also symmetric across the central meridian. A disadvantage to this projection is that its usage is limited to a hemisphere. The scale is also true only along straight lines and otherwise scale, shape, and area distortion increase outward from the center point.

Sphere Wagner IV

I observed that this projection map is a pseudoicylindric projection. The meridians are also portions of ellipses that bulge away from the central meridian. An advantage of this map is that all parallels are straight lines that are perpendicular to the central meridians. Both poles are also displayed as straight lines and the graticule is symmetric across the equator and the central meridian.

World Van der Grinten I

I observed that this projection map is centered on Greenwich and is a polyconic projection. An advantage of this map is that the equator and the central meridian are projected as straight lines of equal length. The graticule is also symmetric across the equator and the central meridian. Its circular look is generally appealing for global maps. A disadvantage of this projection is that its shapes, areas, distances, directions, and angles are all distorted. The polar regions are greatly distorted and exaggerated.
